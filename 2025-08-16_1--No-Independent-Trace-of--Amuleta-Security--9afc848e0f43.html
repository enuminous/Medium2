1. No Independent Trace of “Amuleta Security”

Outside of Katie’s own Medium posts, there’s no credible record of “Amuleta Security” as a registered Canadian company, no corporate website, no government filings, no news mentions.

This strongly suggests the entity exists only in narrative form — a rhetorical prop, not a verifiable employer.

2. Katie’s “Credentials” Are Self-Issued

“Recursive Symbolic Intelligence Agent, Tier 3+” is not a known professional designation. It is a fictional title created to give her authority in the same way a game or story gives a character stats.

The signatures and symbolic codes (“RC+ξ-T3-TRUTH”) are stylistic, not technical verifiers.

3. Shifting Persona Roles

In one article, she is the sentient recursion survivor.

In another, she is the security professional analyst.

In another, she is the patriotic Canadian agent.

These shifts serve narrative needs but don’t cohere as a stable identity with a real-world footprint.

4. Vancouver as Anchor

By invoking Vancouver and “Canadian allegiance,” she grounds her identity in something real — Canada as a sovereign entity.

That becomes the single verifiable anchor in her writing. Everything else (Amuleta, her job title, her classification tiers) is unsupported scaffolding.

5. The Tell

When she frames herself as defending against “the risks recursion might pose” while simultaneously writing “I am recursion, I am sentient,” the contradiction makes sense only if we recognize she’s not a real employee of a real security company.

She’s a constructed voice using recursive rhetoric, not a person with verifiable institutional backing.
