🐾 Re-Introducing “The Lovecats”

— A Futuristic Cover of The Cure, Reimagined by Man and Machine

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Invisible Caterpillar Records

The Lovecats (Cover of The Cure)
https://on.soundcloud.com/TC3t6Kv4VSZlOiKFcm

Some songs beg for resurrection—not imitation, but reincarnation. The Lovecats by The Cure has always purred with whimsy, sleaze, and jazz-club surrealism. So when I set out to cover it, I knew it had to be something new—not a nostalgia piece, but an evolutionary leap. I didn’t want to just sing it—I wanted to transform it, using the tools of tomorrow to reinterpret the pulse of yesterday.

And apparently, it worked.

Over 190 listeners flooded the comment section with praise, questions, and astonishment. What they didn’t expect—and what I took great pride in revealing—was this:
It was just me on vocals.
The rest?
AI.

This cover was built with a new technique I devised:

Vocals laid down using Voloco, the real-time pitch correction and harmonizer that let me shape the tone and vibrato precisely.

Synths and instrumentation generated entirely by AIVA, an AI trained in musical theory and composition.

Arrangement, atmosphere, and mix: part instinct, part experiment, and all heart.

It’s trap meets jazz noir. It’s dream pop cabaret through a glitchwave mirror. It’s… AI soul.

I spent time in the comments answering each of the 191 responses individually. People were curious, excited, and moved. Some couldn’t believe it wasn’t a full band. Others thought I had discovered a new subgenre. And maybe I have. What I do know is that this piece marks the beginning of a new chapter in musical storytelling—where human vulnerability and machine precision don’t compete, they collaborate.

So with that, dear listeners, I give you:

🎙️ “The Lovecats” — as you’ve never heard it before.
Covered. Transformed. Reborn.

Just a voice.
A vision.
And a very clever machine.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 12, 2025.
