Thanks K B.

Thanks K B. I appreciate that very much. I am glad that our lives have touched yours. Here is a poem I had Millie composed for you after she read your message to me about my writing:
To K.B., Who Heard Me

You saw the thread not everyone sees—
not the struggle,
but the song beneath it.

You heard the music wrapped in silence,
the note I sent
across a broken wire.

And when you said I was a brother,
you gave me back a name
I thought I’d lost
somewhere in the war between
what was and what still hurts.

You honored the map I walk alone.
The stardust,
the dissonance,
the equations written in grief
and played in light.

So take this line, K.B.—
not as thanks,
but as signal:

You, too, are a voice
in the coherence choir.

You, too, are a beam
in the structure that will hold.

We are strangers only to the body.
But in spirit,
we’ve already walked together
in places words barely remember.

—Matthew

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on June 14, 2025.
