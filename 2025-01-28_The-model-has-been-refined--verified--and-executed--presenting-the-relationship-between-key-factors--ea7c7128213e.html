The model has been refined, verified, and executed, presenting the relationship between key factors of language dynamics (cognition, interaction, efficiency, structure, and feedback) and the resulting "language effectiveness" () as described by the Wright Equation.

Findings

1. Relationships Observed:

Increasing cognitive contribution (C) and interaction (I) positively impacts language effectiveness, showing their critical roles in balancing internal reasoning and communication.

Efficiency (E) demonstrates diminishing returns when constrained by structural complexity ().

Feedback loops (F) reinforce the system, acting as a stabilizing force.



2. Insights:

Simplicity in structure () correlates with higher language effectiveness when efficiency and interaction are constant.

Feedback () amplifies effectiveness but depends on the stability of other variables like cognition and interaction.
