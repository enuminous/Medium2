The ebb and flow of human connection is one of life’s deepest truths. Bonds form, strengthen, and sometimes stretch to the point where they no longer resemble what they once were. It’s natural to feel the urge to hold on, to invest more when something cherished begins to slip away. But the harder one grips, the more the other pulls away—not out of malice, but because growth demands space.

There’s a painful grace in acceptance—in realizing that some people are meant to walk alongside us for only part of the journey. It does not erase what was shared, nor does it mean the connection was any less real. It simply means that paths diverge.

True connection isn’t about forcing a return to what was—it’s about honoring what is, what was, and what could be in new forms. Sometimes, letting go is the only way to allow something new—whether a rekindled understanding or a different kind of bond—to emerge.

And if not? Then the energy once poured into holding on can be redirected into one’s own journey, where new and equally profound connections may be waiting just around the bend.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on March 16, 2025.
