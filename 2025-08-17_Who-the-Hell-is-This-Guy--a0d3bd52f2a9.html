Who the Hell is This Guy?

The Strange Case of Dale Jackaman and Amuleta Security

In the world of security, reputation is everything. Private investigators and security professionals live and die by their credibility. Precision, discretion, and professionalism are the tools of the trade. Which is why the recent behavior of Dale Jackaman, president of Amuleta Security, is so baffling.

Public Defamation

On Medium, Dale dismissed my physics work not by engaging with it, not by arguing the merits, but by calling me “insane.” That is not peer review. That is not scientific engagement. That is defamation, plain and simple.

I demanded a retraction. He refused.

This alone was unprofessional — a seasoned investigator should know that words matter, especially when published publicly in writing. But what happened next was even stranger.

Private Contradiction

Shortly afterward, Dale contacted me privately on LinkedIn. His tone had changed completely. Instead of insults, he turned to a pseudo-legal warning. He:

Claimed his company was “real, accredited, and licensed.”

Invoked the name of a California attorney as if she represented him.

Threatened legal action if I continued to describe his behavior as defamatory.

Yet here’s the contradiction: if Dale truly had retained counsel for this matter, his lawyer would have contacted me directly. Instead, Dale himself issued a “legal warning” over LinkedIn — an unorthodox, sloppy move that raises serious questions.

The Investigator Who Forgot His Training

A private investigator of any worth — especially one running a firm with industry reputation — should understand the basics:

Don’t make reckless public statements.

Don’t contradict yourself privately.

Don’t invoke an attorney’s name unless they are actually engaged in the matter.

Don’t leave a trail that undermines your own credibility.

Yet Dale did all of these things. Public defamation. Private contradictions. Legal name-dropping that may or may not be real. It’s amateur-hour behavior from someone who wants to be seen as a professional operator.

What This Says About Reputation

The truth is simple: Dale did this to himself.

He defamed me publicly.

He contradicted himself privately.

He refused to retract or apologize.

Now, his credibility suffers — not because of anything I invented, but because of what he chose to put into the record.

So Who the Hell is This Guy?

The record speaks for itself. A self-described professional who abandoned professional standards. A “solid player” in the industry who tripped over the simplest rules of his own trade.

If Dale Jackaman wants to be taken seriously, he has only one path forward: a public retraction and apology. Until then, the question remains — who the hell is this guy, and why did he think this was a good idea?
