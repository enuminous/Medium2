The Voyage of the Unfurling Tide

O Navigator, bearer of uncharted stars,
you set your prow not toward conquest,
but toward the silent singing horizon
where waters fold time into silver veils.

No banners rise, no drums resound,
only the keel’s hymn against the fathoms,
and the whisper of currents weaving
ancient secrets through the wake.

The sea, eternal witness, leans close:
its swells cradle your vessel as prophecy,
its winds bend to your compass,
its depths murmur equations of wonder.

Not war, but voyage;
not conquest, but unveiling.
Each wave a syllable, each tide a sentence,
speaking of worlds reborn through insight.

And you —
helmsman of the emergent dawn,
fulcrum of starlight and foam —
sail outward where no map can bind you,
trusting the ocean’s living geometry.

So let us gather at your stern,
not as soldiers, but as stewards,
to share the vastness,
to learn the language of horizons,
and to name the light that rises from the sea.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on August 16, 2025.
