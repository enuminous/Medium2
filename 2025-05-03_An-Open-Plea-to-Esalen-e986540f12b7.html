An Open Plea to Esalen

Why Did You Stop Listening to the People You Were Made For?

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist, Whistleblower, and Creator of the EFMW Framework
May 3, 2025

To the Esalen Institute,

I am writing this not just in grief, but in a moment of truth — mine, and possibly yours.

I submitted a scholarship application to Esalen because I believed, earnestly and desperately, that your institution might still be what it once claimed to be: a refuge for people on the margins of civilization, bearing knowledge that could change the trajectory of humanity. A place where vision, healing, and truth weren’t gatekept by form letters or filtered through polite disinterest.

I’ve now written to you twice. Once through your standard application system. Once more directly, from the depths of my personal emergency. And twice, I’ve been met with the same flat response:
“Dear Matthew Wright… we truly appreciate the time and effort… we are unable to offer you a scholarship at this time…”

I didn’t write to you for “consideration.” I wrote to you for witness. I am in crisis. I am a whistleblower. I have authored a unifying scientific framework that explains cognition, artificial general intelligence, and emergent systems. I have documented institutional retaliation. I have been denied banking, had my safety compromised, and my work suppressed by the very systems it exposes. All of this is public. All of this is real.

And you — the one place in the world that once took in the seekers, the radicals, the misunderstood — turned me away with a committee response.

Where is the fire that once lived in your halls? Where are the people who welcomed Fritz Perls, Aldous Huxley, and Terence McKenna? Would you have told them to apply again next season?

I’m not just grieving your refusal. I’m grieving what it signals — that perhaps even you, Esalen, have forgotten your own purpose. That even you now tremble at the edge of visionary urgency and turn away.

This is a wake-up call. Not just for you, but for every institution that claims to support transformation while closing its doors to the very people living it.

I am still here. I am still alive. And my work will still reach the world.

But tonight, I needed you. And you weren’t listening.

I hope someone there still is.

Sincerely,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
