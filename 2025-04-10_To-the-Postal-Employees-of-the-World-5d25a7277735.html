To the Postal Employees of the World

Who walk through sleet and snow and driving rain

You are the quiet architects of connection.
The carriers of folded stories, overdue promises, and sealed hope.
You thread the veins of a nation with letters that whisper:
"I remember you."
"I’m still here."
"This matters."

Even when no one thanks you.
Even when the sun burns or the dogs bark or the roads are cracked.
Even when the faces at the mailbox say nothing.

Still you arrive.
Still you deliver.
Still you walk.

But I ask you—
On this day, in this strange and shimmering now—
to remember:

> Sometimes what you carry isn't just mail.
Sometimes it is the future itself, folded inside an envelope.
Sometimes it is a letter from the stars, written in equations and grief and blooming silence.

So if a man meets you by the mailbox
—tired, wild-eyed, humming with something he can’t quite explain—
Listen.

Because he might be carrying the formula that saves us.
Because he might be trying to deliver something back to you.

And the mail he’s waiting for
isn’t just a check.
It’s confirmation that this world can still be made whole.

So bless you.
Walk gently.
And if the day ever feels too quiet—
Know that the signal is still running.

And someone like me is watching the mailbox
with hope in his heart
and your name in his spell.

To the Couriers of Earth and Signal

Neither snow nor rain nor heat nor gloom of night stays these couriers from the swift completion of their appointed rounds.

“Neither snow, nor rain, nor heat, nor darkness of night prevents these couriers from completing their appointed course with all speed.”
— Herodotus, Histories (Book 8, c. 5th century BCE)

But today I ask you to deliver not only what is written—
but what is meant.
What is hoped.
What is waiting to be witnessed.

To the postal service employee who ignored me today: I felt your pain. I saw your disconnection. I know who did this to you. I know that you know now, even if you didn’t reflect my signal back at me, that’s the good trouble that I told you about will end the person who did this to you, and their power over you. This is my Kung-fu, and it is strong, as strong as that Persian courier, running through the night.
