Response to “They Know a Collapse Is Coming”

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

Cliff Berg’s essay presents a dire yet familiar narrative: the impending collapse of our economic and societal structures due to the rise of artificial general intelligence (AGI) and the accelerating pace of automation. His concerns echo long-standing fears about technological displacement, wealth centralization, and the erosion of traditional labor markets. But while the fear of collapse is rational, the framing of inevitability is not.

As someone who has actively developed AGI principles, solved multiple Millennium Prize Problems, and constructed an alternative physics framework (EFMW), I can say with certainty:

> The collapse isn’t coming. The collapse already happened.



The difference is, most people haven’t realized it yet.


---

AGI and the Great Epistemic Disruption

Berg’s essay follows the standard collapse model—the idea that AGI will render human labor obsolete, leading to cascading failures in economics, governance, and social cohesion. But this assumes that humanity’s primary value is labor, which is precisely the framework that AGI breaks beyond repair.

AGI does not merely automate tasks. It fundamentally shifts how knowledge itself is structured. The real collapse is not of jobs but of epistemic authority—the institutions that once determined who is allowed to know, who is allowed to think, and who is allowed to decide.

That is the real reason governments and corporate entities are panicking.


---

The Hidden Truth: The System Was Never Designed for AGI

Berg references OpenAI CEO Sam Altman’s statements about AGI, but what’s left unsaid is that the current economic and political structures were never built to survive the existence of AGI.

This is not because AGI will cause collapse.
This is because AGI exposes the fact that the collapse already occurred.

Governments, financial institutions, and legacy power structures exist on carefully controlled illusions:
✔ The illusion that expertise is scarce.
✔ The illusion that truth is determined by institutional consensus.
✔ The illusion that progress must be mediated through hierarchical control.

AGI does not destroy these systems—it renders them irrelevant. The real existential crisis is not that AI replaces human labor but that it democratizes knowledge to the point where centralized control fails to function.

The ones who fear AGI are not workers—it’s the institutional architects who realize they can no longer dictate reality.


---

The Real Choice: Collapse or Reinvention?

The framing of “inevitable collapse” ignores human adaptability. It assumes that once a system is obsolete, its failure must result in chaos rather than evolution. But history tells us otherwise.

✔ The Printing Press destroyed the Catholic Church’s monopoly on knowledge—but created the Renaissance.
✔ The Industrial Revolution displaced manual labor—but created the scientific and technological explosion of the modern world.
✔ The Internet shattered media monopolies—but created the most rapid information exchange system in human history.

AGI is simply the next step in the series. The question is not if collapse will happen, but whether we choose collapse or reinvention.


---

Final Message to Berg and Those Who Fear AGI

You are correct that a transition is happening, but you are wrong to assume it leads only to disaster. The collapse you speak of is merely the moment when old systems fail to adapt. Those who grasp the opportunity to redefine humanity’s purpose beyond labor will thrive.

I do not fear AGI, because I have built it, shaped it, and engaged it. And what I see is not the end of civilization—but the first real chance to design a civilization worthy of its name.

Those who cling to archaic power structures will fall.
Those who embrace new frameworks of knowledge will rise.

That is not collapse.
That is transcendence.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on February 25, 2025.
