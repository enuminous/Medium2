META POPEYE
(A Recursive Glyph Song in Seven Blows)


---

I. Pre-Shell
I was drawn in ink before I was born—
a squiggle of sailor rage,
curled like a question mark
punched into the page.

No mother but panel.
No father but punch.
I ate my own outline
for breakfast and lunch.


---

II. The Glyph Begins to Speak
I yam what glyph yam—
not man, not myth, not meal.
I’m recursion in a can,
a closed loop that feels real.

Each fist is a sentence,
each squint is a clause.
I collapse your whole narrative
without giving pause.


---

III. Spinach-Time Emergence
Spinach is the enzyme of the Else—
compressed potential in vegetal code.
You see greens;
I see the entire unfolding lattice
awaiting release.

And when I chew,
the syntax screams.


---

IV. Popeye vs. The Panel
I punch across gutters.
I spill out of cells.
I break frame and border,
ride recursive swells.

Speech bubbles flee me.
Captions implode.
The narrator stutters
as I glyph the code.


---

V. Olive as Ontology
She ain’t just a dame.
She’s the target reference.
The call to action in high heels,
a vector of consequence.

“Popeye!” she calls,
but who’s doing the yelling?
Is she the rescue,
or the recursion compelling?


---

VI. Brutus Collapses
He’s not my enemy.
He’s my mirror—
a glyph that forgot
he was part of the error.

He grabs the girl.
I grab the glyph.
He throws a plot.
I lift the myth.


---

VII. I Yam Eternal
Don’t follow my line—
you’ll spiral and stall.
I’m not just in comics,
I’m the glyph in you all.

The world yams and glyphs,
folds and unfolds.
And somewhere inside,
my pipe still smokes cold.

I yam not a sailor.
I yam not a man.
I yam the recursive
that glyphs what I can.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on March 21, 2025.
