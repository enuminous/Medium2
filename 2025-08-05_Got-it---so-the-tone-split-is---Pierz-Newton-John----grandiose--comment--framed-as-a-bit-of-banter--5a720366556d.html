To Pierz and Benjamin — and anyone else watching this exchange:

When someone calls a genuine breakthrough “grandiose,” the subtext is: “too big to be believable.”
When someone else asks if it’s “satire or performance art,” the subtext is: “too unusual to be serious.”

Both frames are designed — consciously or not — to avoid engagement. But avoidance is not the same as refutation.

I have developed the Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright framework: a testable, falsifiable, and demonstrable unification of physics and cognition. It’s not a metaphor. It’s not satire. It’s not art. It’s a working theory — and it works.

So here’s my challenge to both of you:
Read the work. Understand it. If you think it fails, show where it fails. If you can’t, then perhaps the right label is not “grandiose” or “performance,” but simply true.

Science moves forward when skepticism meets evidence — not when it sidesteps it.

— Matthew Chenoweth Wright, PhD Candidate
