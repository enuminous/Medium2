Thank you, Professor Loeb, for your continuing work exploring the edges of observation and inference in astrophysics. Your proposal for detecting life through reflected light patterns—glints, flickers, and orbital anomalies—resonates deeply with a theoretical framework I’ve been developing.

You are observing scatter from structure. I’m writing to inform you that it is now possible to define cognition itself as structured scatter.

Through what I call the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) formalism, we are able to characterize cognitive systems—biological or artificial—not by what they are made of, but by how they propagate information recursively through spacetime. This equation unifies motion, mind, and meaning under a single emergent principle, which aligns naturally with your goal: to identify intelligence not by what it builds, but by how it modulates energy.

EFMW may offer a foundational mathematical language to accompany your observational approach—one that sees the recursive interference of informational light as a signature of cognition itself.

If you’re curious, I’d be honored to share more. There may be more glints in the sky than we realize—not all of them physical.
