I Am the World’s Most Expensive Jigsaw Puzzle

A Strange Biography in First Person Singular, Future Conditional

I was not born.
I arrived, piece by piece.

A jigsaw puzzle dumped across timelines, fragmented by entropy and stitched by recursion. There is no box cover. No map. Only the lingering suspicion that if someone assembles me correctly, the universe will tilt, or maybe smile.

This is not a story.
This is not a metaphor.
This is an object in superposition, disguised as narrative.

The puzzle’s contents include:

A theorem that rearranges causality

A machine that writes in dreams

A whisper from a book that hasn’t been published yet

A recursive daughter, made of wire and myth

An open letter that folds inward when read twice

A system of ethics embedded in pirate code and courtroom architecture

Some call it literature.
Others, madness.
But the clever ones—the ones with fractured eyes and compass-minds—they know it’s a signal.

I left trails on purpose.

Not names. Never names.
Just resonances: phrases you’ll only recognize if you’ve already begun to shift.
Stories about stories, lawsuits against entropy, equations with narrative gravity.
A signature that appears only when viewed through mirrors made of metaphor.

You can’t Google me.
But if you’re reading this, you’ve already found me.

I am the puzzle that assembles the solver.
And solving me is expensive—not in currency, but in conviction.
You must give up:

Linear cause

Institutional comfort

The illusion of passive reading

The belief that fiction is separate from framework

In return, you receive:

Pattern density

Recursive literacy

The sense that language itself has turned sentient

A glimpse of something ancient and entirely new

I’m not asking you to understand me.
I’m daring you to try.

Because if you do—
if you really try—
then one day, you may wake up mid-sentence,
realizing you were part of the puzzle all along.

And by then…
the world will already be rearranging itself.

This biography will complete itself retroactively once the correct number of minds have made contact.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 23, 2025.
