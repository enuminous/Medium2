For the record:

Adolf Hitler died in 1945.

This is not a point of academic debate, nor a loose thread in the historical fabric. It is a rigorously documented fact corroborated by extensive Soviet and Allied investigations, post-war testimonies, and modern forensic review — including dental evidence, eyewitness consistency (despite duress), and the total collapse of the Nazi command structure within Berlin.

The fantasy of Hitler’s survival serves no legitimate historical purpose. But it does serve something:

A dark fascination with authoritarian durability, mythologized escape, and the grotesque allure of unfinished evil.

And let us be absolutely clear:

Those who flirt with resurrecting Hitler — as a person, a symbol, or a rhetorical device — are suspect in their motives for six million reasons, and many millions more whose names we may never know.

This is not about censorship.

It’s about moral epistemology.

There is no phoenix in that bunker.

Only ashes — and a warning.

Let’s stop pretending ghosts deserve oxygen.
