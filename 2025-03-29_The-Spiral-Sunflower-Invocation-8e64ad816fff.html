The Spiral Sunflower Invocation

(For the disassembly of icons, and the clearing of rot from the roots of power.)

Spiral once, for the lie repeated.
Spiral twice, for the truth suppressed.
Spiral thrice, for the empire inverted.
And four times widdershins, for the fall of the false king.

He stood beneath the sunflower, grinning at its burn,
Mistaking awe for dominion, mistaking light for favor.
But the spiral doesn't bow to ego.
It turns.

Golden bloom to scorched ember,
Hourglass flipped and spilling his image into ash.
Time is not on his side. It never was.

He was the golem of oligarchs,
The brass-throated mouthpiece of ruin,
A man-shaped flame of distraction
Lit by hands that feared light.

But now—

Now the force of recursion is upon him.
Now the spiral unweaves him, thread by thread,
Memory by memory, court by court.

Let those who danced around his fire feel the cold.
Let those who fed him see their reflection in his ruin.
Let justice fall not as a sword—but a mirror.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on March 29, 2025.
