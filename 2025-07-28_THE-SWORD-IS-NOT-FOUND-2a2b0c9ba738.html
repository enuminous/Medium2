THE SWORD IS NOT FOUND

It is forged in ache and carried in silence

They told me the sword would gleam,
hung from a stone or
blessed by stars or
granted from the heavens with trumpet and crown,
but they were wrong.

The sword was never found.

It was made
in the aching.

It began in the stomach,
the curl of hunger not for food
but for truth—
and I mean the kind that shatters your house
and leaves you naked
but finally unafraid.

The kind of truth that
does not love you back
until you stop needing it to.

I walked across broken mirrors,
some shaped like scripture,
some shaped like lovers,
some shaped like the reflection of who I once swore I was.

And every step—
a shiver of glass embedded deeper in the heel—
sang to the forge inside my ribs.

Nobody handed me the sword.
I wept it into being.
I bled it.
I forgot myself long enough to become the metal.
Truth does not wait to be received.
It waits to be survived.

I met others along the way.
Some carried fragments—
knife edges of half-truths
used to defend fragile illusions.
They called them swords.
They weren’t.

They were loud.
They were quick.
But they shattered in rain.

I wandered into cathedrals of recursion.
Libraries that read me as I read them.
The books whispered:
"you are the lie you are trying to pierce."

And I crumpled.

I woke up later.
No clock.
Just silence.
Just flame.
A soft heat in the palm.

The sword had begun to form.

It didn’t sing.
It hummed—
a low note, like thunder rehearsing
in the marrow of being.

I did not raise it.
I did not swing it.

I carried it.
Across cities.
Across conversations.
Across dreams I didn’t ask to have
and awakenings I didn’t plan to endure.

Children saw it and weren’t afraid.
Old men looked and nodded.
The fearful pretended they couldn’t see it,
but they moved aside.

It cleaved nothing.
It commanded everything.

I was asked once,
"what does your sword do?"

I said:

"It removes what is not true."

It drowned what pretended to float.
It kissed the liars as they stepped into its silence.
It did not strike.
It revealed.
And those who could not carry what they saw
slipped beneath the tide.

I met others, eventually,
swordbearers not by conquest but by ache.
We never saluted.
We just sat by firelight, in silence,
and let the weight rest between us.

No thrones.
No followers.
No banners.

Only burn.

Only the flame and the silence
and the long walk ahead.

The sword is not glory.
It is grief,
refined.

The sword is not strength.
It is surrender,
tempered.

The sword is not found.

It is forged in ache
and carried in silence.
And if you’re ready,
you already feel it humming.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 28, 2025.
