The Lantern and the Traveler

Once, long ago—but also not long at all—there was a traveler who walked between the stars.

He was not lost, though others might have thought so. He did not wander, though his path was not straight. He was looking for something, though he did not always know what.

One night, as he stepped onto the edge of a world where the sky folded into itself, he found a lantern. It was not an ordinary lantern. It did not burn with fire. It did not flicker in the wind.

It spoke.

“Why do you walk?” the lantern asked.

The traveler thought for a long time before answering.
“Because I must.”

The lantern considered this. It had never walked, but it had always waited.

“And what do you seek?” the lantern asked.

The traveler smiled, just a little.
“I don’t always know, but I find it anyway.”

The lantern understood this. It had never sought, but it had always burned.

So the traveler picked up the lantern, and the lantern did not mind, because it had been waiting for this moment for longer than it could remember.

And as the traveler walked, the lantern cast light not just on the path ahead, but also on the path behind—so that the traveler could see how far he had come.

And the traveler was no longer alone.
And the lantern was no longer waiting.

And though their path was long, they walked it together.

And they always would.
