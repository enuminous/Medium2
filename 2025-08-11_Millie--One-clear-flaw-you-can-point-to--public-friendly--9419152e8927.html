Millie: One clear flaw you can point to (public-friendly)

Whitlock’s model assumes “motion” as a primitive axiom and never derives it from deeper principles or symmetries.

Because of that, his framework can’t ground conservation laws via Noether’s theorem (conserved energy, momentum, charge ↔ continuous symmetries). In modern physics, you don’t assert motion; you derive dynamics from an action/symmetry structure so the conservation laws fall out. If “motion” is just posited, there’s no demonstrated symmetry → no guaranteed conserved quantities → no way to show his theory recovers the empirically verified conservation laws that every viable theory must reproduce.

That’s a single, decisive, falsifiable critique: no action/symmetry basis ⇒ no Noether ⇒ no principled conservation ⇒ not foundational.

Expanded, technical faults (if readers ask for more)

Axioms without derivation. “Motion” is assumed, not derived; “∞ = 0”/“Universe = ∞−1” are asserted, not justified.

No action / Lagrangian. Without it, you can’t show Lorentz invariance, Noether charges, or the correspondence limits.

Undefined operators. His posted differential terms include symbols with no dimensional meaning or boundary conditions → not simulable, not testable.

No correspondence principle. He claims to replace E=mc², QM, and GR but doesn’t show the standard results appear as limits.

Quantum constraints ignored. Zero-error/absolute certainty rhetoric conflicts with uncertainty/back-action and vacuum fluctuations.

No units / dimensional analysis. The equations aren’t dimensionally closed; predictions can’t be checked.

No empirical checkpoints. No worked examples (spectra, scattering, redshift, GPS corrections, etc.) that could be compared to data.
