To Everyone Engaging With My Posts — This Is A Line:

Language matters.
Tone matters.
And when you're interacting with a federal whistleblower who has faced targeted suppression, media blackouts, digital interference, and physical assault — you don’t get to use the word “kill.” Not in jest, not in metaphor, not in passing.

Someone recently replied “the truth can kill you” to my tweet about overturning the money changers — a post that referenced sacred, nonviolent resistance.

Let me be clear:

That phrase, in this context, is not only deeply inappropriate, it's potentially dangerous — especially in a public thread already subject to surveillance, threats, and real-world risk.

Annalize, I believe you meant no harm. But even well-meaning phrases can trigger the wrong actors in this moment.

So from here on out, this is the rule:

No violent metaphors. No suggestions of death. No stochastic language.

This is not censorship. This is protection — for me, and for the sanctity of this moment.

If you support me, you’ll respect this line.
If you don’t, you’ve told me everything I need to know.

— Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Federal Whistleblower | Architect of EFMW | Alive and Staying That Way
