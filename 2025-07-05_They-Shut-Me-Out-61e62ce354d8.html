They Shut Me Out

How Cashli Just Retaliated Against a Whistleblower

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

A few days ago, I published an article on Medium raising serious questions about a fintech app called Cashli — questions about how they handle bank verification, how they communicate with customers, and whether or not their system was being run ethically. I had encountered a deeply suspicious process: after linking my bank account via Plaid, the industry-standard third-party verifier, I was still required to email Cashli directly with my bank details.

That alone raised red flags.

But it gets worse.

I complied. I sent the requested email to their support team to verify my account manually — something no legitimate platform should ever require after already using Plaid. Then I followed up with a second email. In that email, I did two things:

1. I explicitly instructed them not to charge me under any circumstances.

2. I linked to my Medium article, where I publicly raised my concerns about their operations.

I never received a reply.

Instead, I received something else: retaliation.

Locked Out Without Warning

Today, when I attempted to log in to check my account status — the account they pushed me to validate manually — I was greeted with a message:

> “Closed account.
A previously active account has been closed by action of the customer or the Cashli team. If your old account was in good standing, please contact Customer Support for its reinstatement.”

I didn’t close my account.

Cashli did.

And they did it after I raised a public complaint and explicitly told them I was monitoring their actions. Now, I no longer have visibility into what the app is doing. I cannot see if I’m being charged. I cannot confirm if they’ve accessed or retained my banking data. I have no control — and no explanation.

This is not an accident.

This is retaliation.

Why This Matters

Cashli isn't just a random app. It’s one of many emerging fintech platforms being marketed toward low-income and underserved users — people who may not have the resources to fight back if something goes wrong.

Their system now has my bank information. I followed their own verification process. I asked them, in writing, not to charge me. And for that, they responded not with transparency — but with removal.

They took away my access. They silenced a critic. They attempted to erase accountability by locking the whistleblower out of the system.

This isn’t just unethical. It may be illegal.

My Message to Cashli

You have one chance to do the right thing.

1. Reopen my account and show me what actions were taken.

2. Confirm that no charges have been — or ever will be — made to my bank account.

3. Respond publicly and explain why you closed a verified account after receiving a protected consumer complaint.

If you do not, I will escalate this matter to federal regulators and consumer protection watchdogs. And I will not be the last person asking these questions.

To everyone else using Cashli or similar apps:
Ask questions. Document everything. And never let silence replace accountability.

Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist, AGI Researcher, Federal Whistleblower
