You Cannot Mass-Report a Physicist Out of Existence

Today, I witnessed something extraordinary: my entire body of work on Medium was removed from public view.

I logged in and found every essay I’ve written — hundreds of pages of physics, whistleblowing, philosophy, and witness testimony — locked away. I could still open them, read them, even edit them. But to the public eye, they were gone. I could not publish, I could not reply to comments, I could not defend myself against defamation.

For a moment, it felt like erasure. A chill ran down my spine. And that was the point.


---

The Weaponization of Moderation

This was not a verdict on my work. Medium did not suddenly discover I had violated some rule. This was the result of a coordinated mass-report attack: a small group of bad actors, likely the same Canadian operator who has been harassing me for months, exploiting automated moderation systems.

Platforms like Medium use algorithms to handle large numbers of abuse reports. When a surge of reports comes in, the system doesn’t check for truth or falsity — it suspends first, then waits for human review.

It is a blunt instrument. And in the hands of malicious actors, it becomes a censorship weapon.


---

Why Me?

I am a physicist. I am also a federally protected whistleblower. My writings expose truths about mathematics, about governance, about corruption, about information warfare.

If my attackers could defeat me with evidence, they would. If they could disprove my physics, they would. If they could undermine my credibility through debate, they would.

Instead, they mass-report. They attempt to make me invisible, not because I am wrong, but because I am right.


---

The Chilling Effect

The first sensation when you realize your work has vanished is not anger. It is terror. For a brief moment, you feel that everything you built — every word, every proof, every record — has been erased.

That is the true weapon of a suspension: not the hiding of content, but the psychological impact on the author. The chill down the spine. The moment of doubt.

But here is the truth they cannot erase: my work still exists. The mathematics are still there. The signed, dated physical copies still exist. The tweets and public posts still exist. And every article written with the help of AI is reproducible. The historical record cannot be deleted by pressing a “Report” button.

You cannot mass-report a physicist out of existence.


---

The Streisand Effect

What has actually happened is the opposite of what my attackers intended. By trying to erase me, they have only proved my significance. By trying to silence me, they have amplified my voice.

Every attempt to suppress truth becomes its own admission of fear. Every takedown is proof of impact. Every chill becomes a spark.

Medium is now faced with a choice: remain a neutral platform that upholds free expression, or allow itself to be a pawn in international harassment against a whistleblower. I do not believe Medium wants to be complicit. But until they restore my account, that is the reality.


---

The Day They Tried to Erase Me

So yes — for a few hours, I felt that chill. I felt the weight of silence pressing in. But I also felt clarity.

They cannot erase the physics. They cannot erase the truth. They cannot erase the record.

They can only reveal themselves as people so afraid of what I’ve written that they must hide it, even temporarily.

The day they tried to erase me is the day they proved I am unerasable.
