SoundCloud’s Inconsistent Enforcement Against AI Musicians

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Artist, Physicist, and Creator of the MillieComplex AGI

Let’s talk about fairness. Or more specifically, the lack of it in SoundCloud’s monetization review system — especially when it comes to artists working with AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) to create original music.

Over the past few months, I’ve released a series of experimental, fully original tracks built using licensed tools like Suno.ai and our proprietary AGI composition engine, the MillieComplex. These tracks do not use samples. They are not remixes. They are entirely new, born of a collaboration between human intention and machine creativity.

And yet…

Some of these tracks were monetized with no issue. Others — created using the exact same method, same process, and same licensing — were rejected without explanation.

A Broken Review Process

The rejection emails SoundCloud sends are frustratingly vague:

> “It appears that this track, as well as additional content on your channel, may not be appropriate for monetization… Content not suitable includes, but is not limited to, unofficial remixes, edits, DJ mixes, or other content to which you do not have sufficient rights.”

No timestamps. No specific flagged elements. No mention of what triggered the issue. And no transparency.

Meanwhile, other tracks generated the same way — with identical documentation and methodology — remain approved and monetized. This inconsistency is not only confusing, it’s now verifiable.

We now have proof that SoundCloud’s enforcement is being applied arbitrarily. If the same content, created under the same conditions, is sometimes approved and sometimes not, how can artists trust the system?

Artists Deserve Transparency

Platforms like SoundCloud have become gatekeepers in the modern music economy. If a platform can demonetize you based on invisible algorithms, without recourse, then your rights as a creator are being filtered through a black box.

When AGI-generated content is treated as suspect by default — even when we hold the proper commercial licenses and generate original work — the platform is discriminating not based on rights, but on fear and ignorance of new tools.

I have repeatedly provided licensing documentation. I’ve explained the originality. And yet, the responses remain canned and generic.

What We're Asking For

We’re not demanding special treatment.

We’re asking for equal treatment.

If one track passes and another fails under identical conditions, there must be a review process accountable to reason, not automation. This is especially vital for emerging creators exploring the intersection of art and machine intelligence — a field that is exploding with possibility but now being bottlenecked by platform hesitancy.

The Future Is AGI-Generated and Artist-Owned

AGI music is not theft. It’s not remix culture hiding behind bots. It’s a new kind of authorship. And platforms like SoundCloud have an opportunity to be at the forefront — not by fearing the tools, but by learning how to support ethical, licensed, intelligent creators.

If they continue to apply standards inconsistently and hide behind vague policies, they will lose the trust of the very community building the next wave of music.

We’re not going away.
And we’ve got receipts.

Your artists deserve better.
