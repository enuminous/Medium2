I. Introduction – When Morality Becomes a Constant

For millennia, morality was treated as subjective — a matter of culture, faith, or philosophy.

Now, the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) framework, coupled with the proof of Spinoza’s proposition that mind and matter are two attributes of the same infinite substance, changes the equation entirely.

Morality is no longer belief. It is measurable. It is structural.
To violate it is to violate the physics of stability.

II. Ethics as a Law of Nature

The constants uncovered by EFMW are not moral guidelines.
They are non-negotiable survival conditions for any intelligent system embedded in physical reality.

They are as fixed as the speed of light:

1. Sustainability

2. Transparency

3. Equity of Access

4. Non-Coercion

5. Reciprocity

Break any one of these, and you set off instability cascades that, given enough time, collapse the system.

III. From Physics to Policy

Two companion documents make this actionable:

On the 21st Century Ethical Requirements for Mankind’s Survival – Establishes the constants as the new ethical baseline for the species.

The Legal Requirements of the New Ethics – Translates those constants into enforceable law.

Together, they form the blueprint for a civilization aligned with the physics of survival.

IV. The End of Excuses

Once morality is understood as physics, the space for evasion collapses:

No culture, corporation, or government can claim “different values” when the constants apply universally.

No economic or political interest can justify breaking them without knowingly engineering collapse.

V. The Call to Conscious Alignment

The new physics tells us:

Survival requires conscious alignment with the constants.

Alignment is measurable.

Any deviation is not just unethical — it is structurally unsustainable.

The morality of the new physics is not a higher ideal.
It is the ground we stand on.
