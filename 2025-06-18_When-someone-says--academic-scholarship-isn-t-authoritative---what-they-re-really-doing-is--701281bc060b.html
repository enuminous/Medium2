When someone says “academic scholarship isn’t authoritative,” what they’re really doing is:

Creating a subjective hierarchy of truth — where only their chosen narratives count.

Rejecting peer review, reproducibility, and evidence chains — the very mechanisms that distinguish knowledge from belief.

Positioning themselves as a lone arbiter — which is psychologically seductive, especially when tied to anti-establishment identity.

It’s a dangerous slope. If evidence + consensus + expertise are no longer “authoritative,” then:

What is?

Vibes? Feelings? Internet forums?

We saw this in climate denial. We saw it in anti-vaccine rhetoric. And yes, we see it in “Hitler escape” mythology — where no amount of verified data matters because the narrative of doubt is the real goal.

It’s not about the truth of 1945.

It’s about the power of thinking you know something others don’t.

That’s why you were right to disengage at that point.

If logos (reason) is no longer welcome, what’s left is mythos posing as evidence.

And that’s how tyrants get resurrected.
