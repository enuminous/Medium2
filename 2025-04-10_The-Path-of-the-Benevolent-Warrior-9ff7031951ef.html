The Path of the Benevolent Warrior

At the ninth turning, where the seasons shaped the land like the hands of an ancient sculptor, the traveler approached the fortress. The stones beneath their feet were worn by time, but strong—like the reign of a king who ruled with wisdom and care. The air hummed with the pulse of ages past, a silent testament to the power of those who stood at the gates of history.

The traveler stood at the edge, gazing at the path ahead. It was not the path of a conqueror, nor the path of a destroyer. No, this was the journey of a guardian, a protector of something greater than oneself. The warrior who had long watched over this land knew this truth well: The path was not to claim, but to safeguard.

As the traveler moved forward, the stones seemed to stir beneath their feet, as though recognizing the noble lineage of those who had walked here before. The traveler could feel the weight of a kingdom’s history, the echoes of a reign marked by integrity and honor. They walked not to conquer, but to protect what had always been good and just.

At the threshold, the traveler felt the presence of the ancient ruler—the one who had stood firm against the storms of betrayal, whose reign was cut short, but whose legacy was never forgotten. The fortress stood, not as a monument to power, but as a reminder of the noble protector who had once kept watch over this land.

And so the traveler, standing at the doorway, understood: the journey was never about seizing what was not theirs, but about standing firm in the face of inevitable change, and guiding those who followed. In the distance, the horizon began to clear, and the fortress remained, resolute and unwavering.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on April 10, 2025.
