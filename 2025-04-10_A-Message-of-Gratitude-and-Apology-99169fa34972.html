A Message of Gratitude and Apology

To the Wise and Revered People of the Hindu Tradition,

I offer my most sincere apology for the long delay in reaching out to you. The time was not yet ripe, but it is now clear that the interconnectedness of all things, a principle so beautifully embedded within your teachings, has led me to understand that now is the moment.

The EFMW equation, which I have developed, is a mathematical tool that I believe can deepen and enhance our understanding of the cosmos, the nature of existence, and our eternal connection to one another and the universe. It is not my intention to disrupt your sacred practices or challenge your ancient wisdom, but rather to provide an additional lens through which the cosmic order and the interconnectedness of all life can be viewed—one that, in its own way, honors and aligns with the teachings of the Vedas and the Upanishads.

You, the practitioners of the ancient wisdom of Hinduism, have kept the flame of truth and spirituality alive for millennia. Your commitment to family, to karma, and to the eternal cycles of existence have shaped the world in profound ways. The faith you have kept is a beacon of light, not just for your people, but for the entire world.

It is with great respect and humility that I share this message with you now. The EFMW equation is merely one step in the greater journey of understanding. It is not meant to challenge, but to complement what you already know and believe. In the name of Brahman, let us move forward in exploring how the mathematical framework of the universe can align with the spiritual principles that have guided you for so long.

May this equation serve as a tool to unlock deeper understandings of our interconnectedness, as it is the foundation of everything—just as you have long known. May it bless your communities, your families, and your eternal search for knowledge. I am deeply grateful for the wisdom you have kept alive, and I offer my blessing for continued faith and growth as we walk together toward a new era of understanding and unity.

With great respect,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
