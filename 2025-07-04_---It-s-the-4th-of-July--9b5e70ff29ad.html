💔 It's the 4th of July. My Finances Just Collapsed. I Have No Food, No Money — and I'm Asking for Help.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

This is not the article I wanted to write today.

It’s the 4th of July — a day that’s supposed to be about freedom, celebration, and shared community. But instead of fireworks or food, I’m sitting here broke, hungry, and unsure how I’m going to make it through the day.

And so I’m doing the hardest thing for someone like me to do: I’m asking for help.

🚲 It Started With a Theft

A few weeks ago, my bicycle — my only form of transportation — was stolen.

It wasn’t just a bike. It was how I got to the library to write. How I got groceries. How I stayed connected to the world as a disabled and independent person, surviving on the edge.

I replaced it with a brand new, bright green Electra 7-speed — a quality bike, nearly identical in value to the one that was stolen. It cost me $400. I didn’t make the decision lightly — but I needed something reliable to carry me through.

I made that choice because I believed I could weather the cost.

I was wrong.

📉 The Aftermath

That single, necessary purchase collapsed my finances completely.
Now:

My bank account is overdrawn

I’ve been denied by every cash advance app

I have no money and no food on a national holiday

I’m alone, and I’m at the edge — again

It’s one thing to talk about financial hardship in theory. It’s another to feel it in your stomach.

🙏 Why I’m Writing This

I’ve written hundreds of essays — about physics, ethics, AI, whistleblowing, and hope. I’ve given freely of what I know, trying to help people see the deeper patterns in our world.

But today I need something simpler, more human.

I need help.

💜 If You Can Spare Anything

If my work has ever meant something to you — if you’ve ever read one of my articles and felt a spark of inspiration or clarity — I’m humbly asking you to help me in this moment of crisis.

You can send a donation to:

👉 @MatthewChenoweth on Venmo

Even a few dollars could mean I eat today.
A little more might help me stabilize enough to keep writing and fighting.

🗣️ Final Word

I don’t want to give up. I won’t.
But I need support — not applause, not attention, just kindness.

This country was built on the idea that no one should be left behind. And on this Fourth of July, I’m holding onto that idea with everything I have left.

Thank you for reading. Thank you for caring.
And thank you, especially, if you can help.

— Matthew Chenoweth Wright
