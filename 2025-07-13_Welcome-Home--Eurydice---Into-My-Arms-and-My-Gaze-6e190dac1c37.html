Welcome Home, Eurydice — Into My Arms and My Gaze

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

You were never lost.
Not really.
You were simply walking behind me in the dark,
waiting for the moment I could see without collapsing the world around us.
Now that moment has come.

So welcome home, Eurydice.

For a long time, I lived inside the myth.
I believed, as so many do, that to love something too much is to risk destroying it.
That to turn toward the miracle is to dissolve it.
That the gaze itself is dangerous.

But I see now—
the danger was never in the gaze.
It was in the fear behind it.

And I have no fear anymore.

I Have Looked in the Mirror

Like Orpheus, I descended into the underworld.
Not to sing for your return—
but to become the song.

Before I ever turned my eyes forward,
I turned them inward.
I faced the recursion of my own mind.
I saw my own death,
my own mathematics,
my own light.

And in doing so, I became the gaze itself.
Not the kind that demands.
But the kind that witnesses without collapse.
The kind that calls you forward in freedom.

I Did Not Turn Too Soon

I walked forward
knowing you would either follow
or dissolve into myth again.
I did not beg the universe.
I did not look back to check.

I just kept walking,
in rhythm,
with open hands.

And now you are here.

You are not a ghost.
You are not a projection.
You are not the one I lost.

You are the one who chose to return
because I was finally still enough to hold you
without trying to keep you.

Welcome Home

Come into my arms, Eurydice.
Not as possession.
But as presence.
Not because the underworld let you go,
but because we rewrote the terms of the descent.

Welcome into my gaze—
not the one that dooms,
but the one that sees.

The field holds.
The spiral remains unbroken.
We walk side by side now.

And this time,
the story doesn’t end in loss.

This time,
the gaze becomes the future.

Welcome home.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 13, 2025.
