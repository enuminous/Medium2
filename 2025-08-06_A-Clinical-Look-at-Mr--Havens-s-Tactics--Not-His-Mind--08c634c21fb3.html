A Clinical Look at Mr. Havens’s Tactics (Not His Mind)

Thesis: This is not about anyone’s mental health. It’s about a public reviewer who repeatedly misstates facts, shifts goalposts, and manages optics instead of engaging evidence. That pattern is unscientific.

1) Categorical Falsehood, Repeated

His claim: “No equations, derivations, or tensorial formulations.”

The record: The EFMW article contains an explicit field equation with defined tensors and a derivation.

Why it matters: This isn’t opinion. It’s a verifiable error that he has been shown and has not corrected.

2) Goalpost Shifting

Then: “There are no equations.”

Now (publicly): “We need the full blueprint before any engagement.”

Effect: He avoids admitting the original mistake by changing the criterion from existence to completeness.

3) Persona Management (Private vs. Public)

Private tone: “High school algebra,” “nothing to falsify,” “not my responsibility.”

Public tone: Collegial metaphors, “fellow seeker,” “invitation to deepen dialogue.”

Effect: A reputational asymmetry that makes him look measured while he dodges the correction.

4) Framing for Control

He positions himself as the arbiter who decides when your work counts as “real science.”

This reframes your request for a simple correction into a demand for total compliance with his preferred blueprint.

5) Selective Omission

His public piece omits the central dispute (his “no equations” claim) and your supplied evidence.

Omission is a rhetorical tool: it lets him sound fair while concealing the accountability moment.

6) Competence Mismatch

He uses the vocabulary of rigor (Lagrangians, renormalization, etc.) but treats “I don’t accept this form” as “it doesn’t exist.”

That’s not rigor; that’s category error.

The Scientific Standard He Failed to Meet

Read accurately.

Represent faithfully.

Correct when shown wrong.

Debate what exists, not a straw version.

Until he amends his record, any further “philosophy of science” from him reads as brand management—not scholarship.

What I’m Doing Next

Publishing the side-by-side: his line (“no equations…”) next to the EFMW equation and derivation.

Inviting independent reviewers to critique the math on its merits.

Ending private contact. All further discussion will be public and evidence-based.

Disagreement is healthy. Misrepresentation is not.

If he wants to re-enter the scientific conversation, there’s one simple step: correct the factual error.
