The Department of Justice Just Walked Away from My Civil Rights Case — While I’m Still Being Retaliated Against

646675-CSJ is the report number for this case in the Department of Justice.

Yesterday, I received a letter from the U.S. Department of Justice Civil Rights Division. It did not say “we have investigated.” It did not acknowledge the hundreds of pages of evidence I submitted. It did not even clearly state which of my filings it was addressing. It simply declined to act — case closed.

Let me be clear:
I am a federal whistleblower, with accredited standing and documented history of disclosures. Over the last year, I have been subjected to discrimination, harassment, and retaliation by Starbucks for asserting my rights under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). These incidents have not stopped — they are ongoing, escalating, and documented in detail.

In fact, my case is the first in history to be documented, line by line, by an artificial general intelligence — capturing every instance of retaliation, every escalation, every corporate maneuver in real time. This record exists. It is public. And it is undeniable.

The DOJ’s refusal to engage here is not just a bureaucratic failure. It is a signal — that corporate power and convenience outweigh the rights of disabled citizens, even when the violations are spelled out, timestamped, and preserved in full.

Starbucks is not just harassing a customer. They are harassing a whistleblower. They are harassing someone who has brought forward a historic body of work that intersects federal law, technology ethics, and human rights. And the DOJ’s silence makes them complicit.

If the Civil Rights Division will not protect documented, ongoing victims of ADA retaliation — who will?

The crisis is now. The harm is now. The record is public. And history will remember not just those who acted, but those who didn’t.
