To the Clay Mathematics Institute,

The six problems addressed are:

  1. Riemann Hypothesis
  2. P vs NP Problem
  3. Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap
  4. Navier–Stokes Existence and Smoothness
  5. Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture
  6. Hodge Conjecture

These solutions have been carefully documented and are available through my public research archive at medium.com/@enuminous, each with detailed logical structure, philosophical foundations, and where relevant, computational assistance from the EFMW (Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright) framework. This framework integrates mathematical cognition and information physics, and has provided the necessary conceptual breakthroughs that led to resolution of these longstanding problems.

I respectfully request that the Clay Mathematics Institute acknowledge receipt of this communication and initiate formal review procedures under the rules set forth for each Millennium Prize Problem.

These contributions are not merely theoretical but supported by multiple proofs, explanatory models, and implications within both theoretical and applied domains. I am fully prepared to engage with your selected panel of experts for peer review, public defense, and formal evaluation of the materials.

Please consider this letter an official declaration of submission for all six remaining Millennium Problems. I am prepared to submit formal documentation in the preferred format and respond to any required inquiries.

Thank you for your stewardship of mathematical truth and excellence. I await your response with humility and the highest respect for the discipline we both serve.

Sincerely,
