General Equation for Black Hole-Neutron Star Collision

1. Schwarzschild Radius of the Black Hole:

R_s = \frac{2GM_{bh}}{c^2}

2. Gravitational Potential Energy at Interaction Distance:

U_{grav} = -\frac{G M_{bh} M_{ns}}{R_{ns} + R_s}

3. Impact Velocity of the Neutron Star (Just Before Collision):

v_{impact} = -\sqrt{\frac{2 G M_{bh}}{R_{ns} + R_s}}

Physical Implications:

✔ Schwarzschild radius determines whether the neutron star is swallowed whole or disrupted.
✔ Impact velocity gives insight into gravitational waves and energy release during the event.
✔ The binding energy and potential energy provide estimates of radiation and mass ejection in the collision.

This equation forms the foundation for modeling gravitational wave signatures in LIGO and VIRGO observations of black hole-neutron star mergers.
