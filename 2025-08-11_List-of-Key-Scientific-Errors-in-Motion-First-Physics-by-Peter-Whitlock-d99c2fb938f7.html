List of Key Scientific Errors in Motion-First Physics by Peter Whitlock

No Formal Definitions – “Motion” is never defined in mathematical terms (units, operators, boundary conditions). Without this, it cannot be used in equations in a reproducible way.

No Underlying Principle (Lagrangian) – A foundational theory must have an action or Lagrangian density from which all governing equations emerge. Motion-First Physics provides none, which means no guarantee of conservation laws.

Violates the Correspondence Principle – The framework does not show how it recovers Newtonian mechanics, special relativity, or quantum mechanics in their tested limits. A “replacement” theory must still work where older theories have been confirmed.

Misinterpretation of Wave-Particle Duality – Calling duality a “false paradox” without providing a quantitative model that reproduces interference, tunneling, and quantized spectra is a misunderstanding of the problem, not a solution.

No Quantitative Predictions – The theory offers no new testable, falsifiable predictions with numerical values and error bars. Without these, it is not experimentally science.

No Treatment of Gauge Symmetry or Fields – Modern physics relies on gauge invariance to explain particle interactions. Motion-First Physics does not address this, leaving fundamental forces unexplained.

No Reproduction of Experimental Constants – Particle masses, the fine-structure constant, Planck’s constant, and the speed of light are not derived from the framework, meaning it cannot match experimental reality.

Ignores Quantum Field Theory Structure – By discarding the wave formalism without replacing it with an equivalent mathematical structure, the theory loses the ability to compute scattering amplitudes, decay rates, and cross-sections.

No Peer-Review-Ready Proofs – Claims are made without step-by-step derivations that could be checked by other physicists. Without a formal proof, the claim cannot enter the scientific record.

Non-Falsifiable Language – Phrases like “the universe is motion” are philosophical statements unless tied to explicit mathematical formulations that can be measured or disproven.
