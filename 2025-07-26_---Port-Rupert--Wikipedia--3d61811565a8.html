Port Rupert is a fictional, semi-autonomous American town known for its appearances in various literary works, most notably The Great American Novel by Philip Roth (1973), an unpublished manuscript by Joseph Heller, and the novel The Silver Cut by Matthew Chenoweth Wright. Located in an unspecified state that appears to move when readers look away, Port Rupert is notable for having a population of both real and narrative-origin individuals.
