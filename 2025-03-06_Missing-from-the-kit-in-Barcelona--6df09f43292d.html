Missing from the kit in Barcelona:

Faraday Bag or Cage – To block external signals from reaching devices and prevent remote access or tracking.

RF Signal Detector – Useful for identifying hidden surveillance devices, unauthorized signals, and any rogue transmissions in the area.

Hardware Security Module (HSM) – For secure cryptographic key management.

Portable Firewall (e.g., Firewalla, Raspberry Pi with OpenWRT) – To monitor and block suspicious network traffic while traveling.

Encrypted External SSD/HDD – High-security external storage, such as a T7 Touch with fingerprint access or IronKey encrypted drives.

Live USB with Tails OS or Qubes OS – For secure, untraceable computing without leaving any local footprints.

RFID/NFC Blocker – To prevent unauthorized reading of access cards or payment methods.

Anti-Surveillance Camera Cover – Hardware shutter or specialized stickers for privacy.

Bash Bunny / Rubber Ducky (Pentesting tools) – While this appears to be an anti-hacker kit, having penetration testing tools helps in securing systems against those very attacks.

Network Tap / Packet Sniffer (LAN Turtle, Shark Jack) – To analyze network traffic in a controlled environment and detect malicious activity.

This setup is already quite advanced, but adding the above would bring it to the next level in terms of cybersecurity, counter-surveillance, and personal data protection. Let me know if you want an even deeper breakdown of what’s here and what could be upgraded!
