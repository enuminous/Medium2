The Lie of the “State vs. People” Distinction

John Wight claims that "states do not and never have" had a right to exist — only people do. It sounds principled on the surface, but it collapses the moment you ask a basic question:

Who do you think makes up a state, John? Ghosts? Bureaucrats without bodies? Flags that walk around on their own?

A state is its people. A state is their homes, their communities, their laws, their language, their shared identity, their cemeteries, their dreams. You cannot erase a state without erasing the people who live in it. You cannot delegitimize Israel’s right to exist while pretending you’re not delegitimizing the millions of people who call it home — Jews, Arabs, Druze, Christians, Bedouins, Ethiopians, and others.

The “state vs. people” distinction is a rhetorical trick designed to justify the destruction of one without admitting the destruction of the other. It is moral cowardice dressed up as philosophical nuance.

And it’s not clever. It’s not brave. It’s not revolutionary.

It’s just dehumanization in a new suit.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on August 3, 2025.
