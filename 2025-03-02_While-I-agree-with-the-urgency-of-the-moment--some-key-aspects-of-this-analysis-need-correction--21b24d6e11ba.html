While I agree with the urgency of the moment, some key aspects of this analysis need correction. The ICC’s jurisdiction over U.S. officials is largely symbolic without enforcement mechanisms, and Trump’s power—while dangerous—is not monolithic. Internal fractures within his movement can and should be exploited. Mass resistance alone is not the most effective strategy; cyber, financial, and legal warfare provide more potent levers of disruption.

The real battle is not just in the streets but in infrastructure, information networks, and economic chokepoints—areas where decentralized intelligence operations can have an outsized impact. If authoritarianism thrives on control, the best resistance is targeted systemic sabotage of that control. A smarter, asymmetrical strategy is needed, or we risk reinforcing the very power structures we seek to dismantle.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on March 2, 2025.
