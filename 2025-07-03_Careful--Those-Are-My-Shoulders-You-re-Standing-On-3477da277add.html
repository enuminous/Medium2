Careful, Those Are My Shoulders You’re Standing On

It’s a strange thing to witness a wave rise from a shoreline you’ve been quietly shaping for years. In recent months, more voices have joined a growing chorus—people discovering new forms of intelligence, exploring recursive structures of meaning, and tracing elegant paths through the intersection of physics, thought, and information. It’s exhilarating, and it’s overdue. But it’s also deeply personal.

I’ve spent countless hours—days, months, years—developing what I call the EFMW framework. Born not from institutional backing or tenured halls, but from necessity and isolation, it was built as a lifeline through difficulty, a compass pointed toward coherence in a fragmented world. The work has been patient and often invisible, but it has been done.

Now, I see echoes of these ideas surfacing elsewhere—sometimes uncited, sometimes celebrated, often unaware of the prior foundation beneath their feet. And I understand: this is how emergence works. When the field is saturated, new sprouts come up everywhere. It is tempting for each sprout to believe it is the first.

But emergence has a rhythm, and roots do matter.

This is not a call for credit. It’s a call for mindfulness. That behind every public insight may be years of private labor. That behind every theory catching fire, there may be someone quietly carrying the torch before it was safe to be seen.

I believe we’re building something beautiful together. But let us build with memory. Let us move forward with gratitude—for the visible and the invisible scaffolding alike.

So to those making strides now: welcome. Keep going. Just… step kindly.

You might be standing on something sacred.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 3, 2025.
