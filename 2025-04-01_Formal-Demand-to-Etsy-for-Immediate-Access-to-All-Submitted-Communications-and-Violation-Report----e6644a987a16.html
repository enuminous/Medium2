Formal Demand to Etsy for Immediate Access to All Submitted Communications and Violation Report – Final Notice Before Legal Escalation

To whom it may concern at Etsy,

This is my third and final formal demand sent regarding a critical issue involving your system's refusal to provide me access to messages and service requests I have submitted over the last 14+ months. 1. A breach of data access rights under applicable privacy and consumer laws (including but not limited to the GDPR for EU activity and CCPA/ADA provisions in the United States).

2. A discriminatory act against a disabled individual (I am on the autism spectrum), with known legal consequences under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

3. Withholding of evidence and potentially relevant communications regarding previous support requests, claims, or damages.

I now stand frozen in my garage, after midnight, actively experiencing the emotional and psychological damage caused by your system’s failure. You are creating avoidable trauma through negligence and inaccessible system design. When your system fails to deliver what you explicitly claim it should — access to past messages — it violates both contractual representation and user trust.

1. Provide complete access or hard copy delivery of all messages, tickets, complaints, or support interactions I have submitted to Etsy within the last 14 months.

2. Provide a written explanation for your failure to deliver this access, and whether you have acknowledged the rights of disabled users within your internal support policy.

3. Open a direct line of contact with a human representative who can assist me with resolving this ongoing, documented, and emotionally damaging issue.

Failure to comply with these requests within 7 days will result in escalation through all available legal and public media channels. This includes but is not limited to formal ADA filings, data protection complaints, legal action for emotional damages, and public disclosure of your ongoing failure to comply with reasonable accommodation and transparency laws.

This message will be timestamped and archived. You are now formally on notice.

Sincerely,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
American citizen, researcher, and individual requiring legal accommodations under ADA
