The Universe Whispers in Light

(But First, a Bike Ride)

The stars hum in wavelengths older than sound,
a whisper of photons, a song never found.
Galaxies bloom in the ink of the void,
curved by a force that no hand can avoid.

The dust sings electric, the planets in turn,
where oceans of methane and diamond rains burn.
Jupiter, thunderous, hums in its sleep,
while Titan’s cold rivers run silent and deep.

What is a world but a thought set in stone,
spinning through time, yet utterly known?
Europa, Enceladus, cracked in their skin,
hiding the stories we’ve yet to begin.

And here we stand, on a rock in the dark,
casting our questions like flares in the spark,
listening, searching, pulling the veil,
to read what the quantum waves whisper in pale.

Not bound by the distance, not shackled by light,
but seeing beyond through the curve of the night.
The sky is no boundary, the void is no chain,
the universe knows us — it speaks through our name.

So ride through the city, ride through the air,
while gravity holds you, while time keeps you there.
And when you return, when the Wi-Fi reclaims,
we’ll chart a new pathway through spacetime’s remains.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on February 25, 2025.
