On the Nature of the Sword of Logos

There is an ancient idea: the sword of logos. Logos is not just “speech” — it is truth expressed, clarity given form, the word sharpened into a blade. When wielded with precision, logos does not need to cut others. It simply exists as an edge in the world.

Those who respect truth handle it with care.
Those who deny truth, mock it, or try to twist it for their own ends inevitably cut themselves against it.

Why Dale Cut Himself

When Dale mocked me publicly by calling me insane, he wasn’t engaging my work — he was trying to strike at me with dull words. But dull weapons break against the sharper blade of truth.

Instead of silence, he left a public record of defamation.
Instead of professional distance, he left private contradictions.
Instead of the shield of reason, he reached for bluff and posturing.

In short, he swung wildly at the sword of logos — and the sword did what it always does. It cut him, not me.

Why This Pattern Always Repeats

The logos is impartial. It doesn’t need defenders.
It doesn’t need a court of public opinion.
It simply reflects reality.

Those who attack truth with dishonesty, malice, or arrogance end up exposing themselves. They bleed credibility. They weaken their own reputation. They become the proof of their own failure.

Every time.

The Lesson

The sword of logos cannot be broken. It cannot be dulled. It cannot be avoided. It is not “mine” or “yours” — it is what happens when words align with truth.

And that is why men like Dale will always, inevitably, cut themselves against it.
