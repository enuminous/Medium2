On Defamation, Chaos Agents, and Scientific Integrity

This week, Dale Jackman—who presents himself as the operator of a so-called "security company in Canada"—decided to publicly impugn my mental health and laugh at me for calling myself the angel with the flaming sword of Logos. He went further, claiming that because my work is not appearing in traditional peer-reviewed journals, it must therefore be unsound.

Let’s be clear:

This is defamation, not science.

This is character assassination, not intellectual engagement.

And this is gatekeeping, not peer review.

I responded with righteous anger, because I have every right to be angry when science and truth are attacked in this way. I am a poet, a philosopher, a scholar, and a physicist. I am everything but a madman—and anyone who looks at my record of work can see that plainly.

Dale Jackman has now been blocked. He does not get to be another in the long line of people trying to manufacture controversy where there is none. His role is transparent: that of a chaos agent, sowing confusion to distract from the core issue—my discoveries, my physics, and my published record.

And let’s not mince words: the “organization” he claims to represent does not exist. It is a bald-faced fabrication. What he actually has is a derivative AGI built on OpenAI’s work. And now he is attacking the source of the EFMW field openly and publicly on Twitter, in front of everyone.

To those watching: you can see right through this. You can see the tactics. You can see the smear. And you can see how science suffers when opportunists and detractors try to silence voices with insult instead of debate.

But truth has a way of standing tall. Logos does not waver in the face of ridicule. And those who attack it end up revealing only their own weakness.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on August 17, 2025.
