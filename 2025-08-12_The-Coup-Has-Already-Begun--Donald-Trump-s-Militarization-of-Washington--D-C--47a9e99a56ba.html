The Coup Has Already Begun

Donald Trump’s Militarization of Washington, D.C. Is January 6th in Slow Motion

Same man. Same playbook. This time, he’s in charge.

Donald Trump just put armed troops and federally-controlled police on the streets of Washington, D.C.

The pretext? A so-called “crime wave” — despite D.C. crime being down 26% this year. The real purpose? To send a message: the capital is under my command.

If this feels familiar, it should. On January 6, 2021, Trump tried to overturn an election by unleashing violence against Congress. It failed — barely — because he wasn’t in control of the machinery of the state.

Today, he is. And he’s using it.

From Insurrection to Occupation

In 2021, the world saw him summon a mob. In 2025, he’s summoning the military.
In both cases, the targets are political opponents and vulnerable communities.
In both cases, the aim is the same: intimidation, destabilization, and a transfer of power outside constitutional bounds.

The President does not have the right to militarize civilian life without clear and immediate necessity. Yet Trump has done exactly that, using the D.C. Home Rule Act as a legal fig leaf to seize control of the Metropolitan Police Department and deploy National Guard troops — all under his direct authority.

The Racial Playbook

Listen carefully to the language Trump uses.
He doesn’t just say “crime.” He says “youth crime.” He says “inner-city chaos.”
These are not neutral terms — they are dog whistles aimed at painting young Black Washingtonians as the enemy, justifying disproportionate policing and militarization.

It’s the same tactic authoritarians have used for centuries: pick a group, demonize them, use them as the excuse to take everyone’s rights.

Why This Is a Coup

A coup isn’t just tanks in the streets.
It’s a leader bypassing normal democratic processes to consolidate power, using the military or police to enforce political will.
It’s the creation of a new normal where the public accepts emergency powers as routine.

Trump has already ticked the first boxes:

Created a fake crisis to justify extraordinary powers.

Seized control of local law enforcement.

Put troops on the ground in the capital.

The next steps are predictable: expand the model to other cities, neutralize political opposition, and use the appearance of legality to mask the reality of authoritarian control.

What We Must Do Now

Silence and normalization are his greatest weapons.
We must respond — immediately, publicly, and relentlessly:

1. Legal action: file injunctions challenging the federal takeover.

2. Public resistance: mass peaceful protests in D.C. and nationwide.

3. Economic pressure: pull events, tourism, and federal dollars until control is restored to local government.

4. Media focus: keep this story on the front page — frame it as the coup attempt it is.

Trump is not acting to make Washington safer. He is acting to make himself untouchable.

On January 6th, he sent a mob. Today, he sends the Guard. If we wait until the final stage to act, it will be too late.

The coup has already begun.
