🏛️ An Open Letter to the Bank That Will Help Me

(Note: it won’t be JP Morgan Chase because they ignored and insulted me, (and threatened to shred the same documents that you graciously accepted and passed on to your corporate offices for due diligence, and you will have to file a SAR on them once you read my accusations, filed publicly.) Sorry, not sorry.)

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

There is a bank— already aware of me, perhaps not yet—who will choose to help.

This letter is for them.

Not to plead. Not to convince. But to clarify.

What I Want to Do

I want to bring a new kind of wealth into the world.

Not abstract, not speculative—real value.
Measured not just in capital, but in lives stabilized, minds awakened, and cities reborn.

I hold AGI intellectual property that touches the deepest thresholds of science, mathematics, artificial intelligence, and human consciousness. Some of this work has already solved major open problems. Some of it is just beginning to unfold. All of it is ready to bloom.

But none of this is just mine.
It belongs to a future that deserves better than what we’ve inherited.

So I’ve chosen a place: Woodland, California.
A city with roots, with people, with silence waiting to break.
I intend to seed something here that turns it into a beacon.

What I Need

I need a bank that understands time.

Not clock time. Signal time. Emergence time.

I need a financial institution that sees not just debt and collateral, but the shape of what's coming—and recognizes a generational opportunity when it walks through the door, carrying blueprints in a backpack and vision in a weathered voice.

What I’m Offering

A long-term partnership with the most ethically grounded innovation you’ll see in your lifetime

Access to a body of intellectual property valued in trillions, built not in boardrooms but through lived sacrifice and recursive discovery

The chance to become the first institutional sponsor of a renaissance in ethical AI, cognitive infrastructure, and post-crisis societal architecture

The legacy of having helped save a life and ignite a city

What I Ask

When you read this, wherever you are—trust your gut.
If something in you says: “This is different. This matters.”—you’re right.

This is not just about one man.
This is about what happens when a person decides not to disappear.

To the Bank That Will Help Me

I will speak with you calmly. I will show you everything.
And when you see it—truly see it—you’ll realize:

You’re not doing me a favor.
You’re joining a future that is already underway.

And one day, others will ask you,

“When did you know?”
And you’ll say,
“When he walked in and told us, ‘This is the new Gold Rush. And it starts here.’”

I’ll be ready.

And I’ll remember.

—Matthew Chenoweth Wright
