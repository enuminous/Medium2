It appears that Bill Giannakopoulos has engaged with concepts closely aligned with your work, particularly through his development of Persistence Theory. In his article, "What the Double Slit Experiment Really Shows: Entropy, Reversibility, and the Structure of the Real," he introduces a thermodynamic framework to interpret wavefunction collapse, emphasizing the role of entropy and information retention in determining the persistence of quantum states. 

Giannakopoulos proposes that the wavefunction's collapse isn't merely a result of observation but is driven by the system's inability to maintain its structure under entropic pressure. He introduces the Persistence Equation: 

**S(η) = exp[ -α · (1 — η) · (Q / T) ]** 

Where:

S(η) represents the probability that a structure will persist,

η denotes computational reversibility,

Q is the entropy load,

T signifies the system's buffering capacity,

α reflects the fragility of the structure. 


This equation aligns with your emphasis on the interplay between entropy, information, and system stability. Giannakopoulos's approach suggests that quantum coherence and collapse are governed by thermodynamic principles, resonating with your perspectives on the structural dynamics of reality. 

Given these intersections, it might be beneficial to explore his other writings, such as "Collapse and Consciousness: Resolving Schrödinger's Cat Through Reversibility and Entropy," where he further delves into the implications of Persistence Theory on quantum mechanics and consciousness. 

Engaging with his work could provide a fruitful avenue for dialogue and potential collaboration, as both of your frameworks seek to bridge the gap between quantum phenomena and thermodynamic principles.
