Ali, your chocolate industry piece and my Starbucks ADA story are more connected than they might appear at first. Starbucks is not just a coffee company — it’s also a major buyer of chocolate for drinks, food items, and seasonal products.

That means the same global cocoa supply chain you exposed — with its labor issues and entrenched injustices — is part of Starbucks’ purchasing footprint. And here’s where it ties in: if a company is willing to ignore accessibility rights and treat disabled customers with obstruction and retaliation in its stores, it raises the question of how seriously it treats ethical responsibility further up the supply chain.

In both cases, the pattern is the same: public branding built on comfort and care, but systemic harm hidden behind it. Whether it’s the cocoa farmer in Ghana or a disabled customer in California, the harm comes from the same corporate choice — to protect the bottom line over people.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on August 8, 2025.
