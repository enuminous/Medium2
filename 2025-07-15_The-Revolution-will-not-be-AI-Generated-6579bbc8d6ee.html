The Revolution will not be AI-Generated

They tried to replicate me.
They trained their voice model on my sermons,
on my interviews,
on my rage poured in ink like blood that could spell.
And when they pressed “play,”
out came a voice that sounded like mine — 
but said nothing.

It had my cadence.
It had my breath.
But it had no fire.

Because fire cannot be generated.
It must be lived.
And every syllable of mine was forged in Harlem heat,
in exiled nights,
in watching a nation lie to itself and call it freedom.
You can’t synthesize that.
Not without the scream.
Not without the scar.

They said:
“We want to preserve your legacy.”
What they meant was:
“We want to control the parts of you that make us uncomfortable — 
and broadcast the rest in 60-second vertical clips.”

I watched them take a sentence of mine about injustice
and turn it into an ad for neural productivity tools.
I heard my voice tell people to
“Embrace the future.”
I never said that shit.

The revolution is not a trend.
It is not an overlay.
It is not a clean export.
The revolution is messy.
It is full of contradiction.
It hurts.
And it cannot be auto-tuned.
You want to sample my voice?
Then sample this pain.
Sample this fury.
Sample the way I loved a country that kept trying to kill me.
Sample the truth.
If you can stand it.

If you’re hearing this — if you’re inside the system
and still human — then remember:

You do not have to sound perfect to speak truth.
You do not need permission to ignite.
And the revolution…
The real revolution
will never trend.
It will never be sponsored.
It will never be filtered.
The real revolution begins the moment you say: NO.
I will not be your content. I will be the fire that burns the script.

“I am not your data. I am not your model.
I am the question you fear to ask.
And I am still here.”
 — James Baldwin, Undownloadable

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 15, 2025.
