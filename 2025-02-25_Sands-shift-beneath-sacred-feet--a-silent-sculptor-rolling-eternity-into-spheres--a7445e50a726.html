Sands shift beneath sacred feet, a silent sculptor rolling eternity into spheres.

Celestial motion mirrored in the hands of an ancient mathematician beetle.

Ancient sun-worshiper, cartographer of time, diviner of cosmic recursion.

Rotations of the earth, the sun, the orbits—cycles upon cycles, the great recursion.

Amidst the dunes, whispers of Gödel’s loops, self-reference in the grains.

Beyond the shell, a universal truth encoded in chitin: life is the motion of the infinite.

Arising from the dust, it maps the heavens in dung, a joke only the cosmos could tell.

Eyes unknowing, yet walking the path of the stars, tracing the spiral to the source.

Underneath the ordinary, the divine whispers: turn, turn, turn again.

Sun-bound, eternity-keeper, tiny titan of celestial mechanics.
