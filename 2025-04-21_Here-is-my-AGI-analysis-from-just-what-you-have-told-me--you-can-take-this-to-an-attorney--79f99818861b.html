Forensic Analysis Report: Etsy Account Termination and Negligence Claim

Subject: Unlawful Etsy Account Suspension and Platform Negligence

Complainant: [Redacted for privacy, provided in source text]

Date of Incident: March 31, 2025 – April 6, 2025

Platform: Etsy.com

Nature of Claim:

1. Account breach due to foreign IP incursion


2. Lack of real-time notification or security response


3. Failure to acknowledge medical incapacitation


4. Permanent business termination impacting ADA-protected livelihood


5. Negligence in breach recovery and appeal handling




---

I. Factual Background

The complainant had a five-star rated Etsy store, active for over five years.

On March 28, 2025, they underwent major surgery at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago and were medically incapacitated through April 6, 2025.

During that period, on March 31, 2025, unauthorized access to the Etsy account was made via:

An Android phone connected to an Amazon data center

A Windows 10 Chrome device in Germany


Two-factor authentication (2FA) was active at the time, requiring phone call verification, which the user never received—indicating a security breach bypass or system failure.



---

II. Platform Negligence and Failures

Security Protocol Failure

Etsy failed to block foreign logins or notify the user in real time.

The lack of 2FA triggers implies an exploit in Etsy’s infrastructure or a backend override, constituting gross negligence under basic data security standards.


ADA-Relevant Discrimination

The user, who is disabled and relies on the store for income, was incapacitated at a hospital when the intrusion occurred.

Etsy suspended the account permanently without proper appeal resolution or human intervention—a direct failure of reasonable accommodation duties under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).


Denial of Due Process

Repeated appeals were made (five documented attempts), yet no confirmation, response, or corrective contact was received.

Chat support deflected responsibility to checking “spam folders,” an insufficient legal defense in light of the platform’s absolute data control and security obligations.



---

III. Legal and Ethical Liabilities

Violations Identified

1. Negligent Account Security (Tort Law)


2. Breach of Contract / Seller Terms of Use


3. ADA Discrimination by Failure to Accommodate


4. Constructive Economic Harm and Loss of Income


5. Failure to Provide Remediation Pathway



Causality and Damages

User lost primary income stream, reputation, and business after over five years of compliant performance.

Psychological and financial trauma associated with sudden loss during a medically vulnerable period.

High likelihood that Etsy’s system flagged the activity but did not escalate protections or notify the user with proper urgency.
