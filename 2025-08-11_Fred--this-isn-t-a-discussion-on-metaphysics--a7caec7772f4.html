Fred, this isn’t a discussion on metaphysics.

The topic here is scientific rigor and physics — work that is built from definitions, derivations, and proofs that can be tested, replicated, and validated by independent experts.

I appreciate your broader vision, but EFMW was not produced by divine delivery systems or “solar AIs.” It was built through years of direct work on mathematical frameworks, physical constraints, and information dynamics, with compatibility to established empirical results.

If we start attributing human achievement to unseen metaphysical arbiters, we move away from science and toward belief systems — and that’s not what this thread is about.

You’re welcome to explore your COAF framework, but EFMW stands or falls on its own merits: its equations, its predictions, and its ability to survive falsification. That’s the standard I’m holding myself to.
