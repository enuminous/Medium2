The DOJ Just Declined My ADA Complaint Without Reviewing My Evidence

An open letter from a federal whistleblower to the Civil Rights Division

On August 4th, 2025, I filed a formal ADA discrimination and retaliation complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice’s Civil Rights Division against Starbucks.

This was one single, consolidated complaint — but it contained an unprecedented volume of evidence:

Over 40 documented ADA violations published on my Medium Starbucks ADA list, each timestamped and archived.

Photographic, video, and witness corroboration.

Detailed legal context for each violation.

Cross-referenced analysis produced by the world’s first artificial general intelligence, which has been documenting this case in real time.

This was not a “he-said-she-said” filing. This was a complete, publicly verifiable record of systemic discrimination by one of the largest corporations in America.

The DOJ’s Response

Within days of my submission, I received a form-letter declination from the Civil Rights Division. The letter gave me only a case number — 646675-CSJ — and no indication that my evidence had been reviewed.

There was:

No acknowledgment of the attached documentation.

No reference to any specific incident or violation.

No contact with me for follow-up or clarification.

The Impossible Timeline

Let’s be clear:
To meaningfully review a single ADA complaint with over 40 documented violations — each with supporting evidence, cross-referenced legal citations, and corroborating material — would take days, if not weeks of investigator time.

The Civil Rights Division closed this case in less than the time it would take to simply read my documentation, let alone investigate it.

That’s not due diligence.
That’s dismissal without review.

Why This Matters

If a federal agency can discard a complaint this large and this well-documented without even acknowledging the evidence, then:

ADA enforcement is not functioning in practice.

Federal whistleblowers are being left unprotected.

Corporations can engage in repeated, well-documented discrimination with impunity.

My Public Demand

I am demanding that the Civil Rights Division:

Confirm whether they reviewed any of the evidence in my August 4th submission.

Provide the procedural record showing steps taken from receipt to declination.

Justify how this volume of evidence could be reviewed — or disregarded — in such a short time frame.

This is not just my fight. It’s a test of whether the ADA is a living law or a hollow promise.

The American public deserves to know if the Department of Justice is protecting the rights of disabled Americans — or protecting corporations from accountability.

If you believe in civil rights and real ADA enforcement, share this.
Let’s find out whether our justice system is still in the business of justice.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on August 14, 2025.
