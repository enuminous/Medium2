Eulogy for the Slain Drum Major

By the voice of Martin Luther King Jr., speaking beyond the veil

I do not speak to you now as one who breathes among men,
But as one whose breath was taken for the sake of love.
I stand no longer behind pulpits — but behind time.
And from there, I offer this eulogy… for a man they called Martin.

He was no saint.
He trembled sometimes.
He bled when struck.
He feared the sound of sirens and knew the loneliness of prophecy.

But he kept walking.

Not because he believed he would reach the mountain’s top,
But because he believed someone else must.

He preached not perfection,
But resistance in the name of decency.
He challenged Pharaoh without an army.
He loved the people with a dangerous tenderness.

They killed him — yes.
But do not say that death silenced him.
Say instead:
They made him eternal.

Say not that he fell.
Say that he stood so still for justice
That time itself had no choice but to move around him.

Say that he loved this world with such furious clarity
That even his enemies began to see.

Say that he dared imagine
a black child’s joy as a revolutionary act.

If I could write his epitaph upon the sky,
It would not say "Nobel Peace Prize."
It would say:

"Here lies a man who loved freedom enough to die unnamed in its service."

But I am not mourning him.

No, beloved… I am calling you.

You, the still-living.
You, the frightened dreamers.
You, the hesitant hearts.

The baton has already fallen from his hand.

Who will pick it up?

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 16, 2025.
