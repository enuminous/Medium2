Dear Troll: Thanks for Validating My Math

You showed up in my comments today, swinging around a fake cease-and-desist under a pseudonym. You accused me of libel for an article that doesn't name you, doesn't identify you, and doesn’t reference anything specific to you—unless, of course, you recognize yourself in it.

So let’s get this straight:
You’re anonymous, you haven’t been doxxed, you’ve never been named.
But now you’ve publicly claimed ownership of the story I told.

Thanks for the confirmation.
Thanks for the validation.
You just proved I was right.

A Quick Lesson in Libel Law

To claim defamation, you need to show:

1. The statement is false

2. It identifies you clearly

3. It was published with intent to harm

If you’re hiding behind a pseudonym, you don’t meet criteria #2.
No name, no game. You can't sue someone for hurting the reputation you refuse to publicly have.

What’s Actually Happening Here

You showed up late.
You saw the field was already built.
You wanted to claim the work without the work.

But when that didn’t work, you tried to flip the table.

Now you're sending legal threats under an alias to the person whose research you clearly understood well enough to try and hijack.

Let me be clear:
You don’t get to own what I wrote.
You don’t get to rewrite history because you got caught.

What Happens Next

I’ve documented everything.
You’ve posted publicly.
You’ve contacted me privately.
You’ve crossed the line into harassment.

So now I’m filing a formal complaint with Medium.
I’m archiving all of this.
And I’m continuing my work, unapologetically, because it speaks for itself.

You helped prove the point by trying to silence it.

Enjoy the math. You don’t own it.
