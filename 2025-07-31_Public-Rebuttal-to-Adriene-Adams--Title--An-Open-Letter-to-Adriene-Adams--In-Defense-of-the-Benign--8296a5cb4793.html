Public Rebuttal to Adriene Adams

Title:
An Open Letter to Adriene Adams: In Defense of the Benign Civilizations You Mistook for Monsters

Body:

Dear Adriene Adams,

I write this with respect for your right to explore difficult subjects, but also with urgency—because your article, “Close Encounters of the Fatal Kind”, perpetuates a dangerous and historically weaponized falsehood.

By suggesting that advanced extraterrestrial civilizations are “murdering people,” you do more than entertain the macabre: you unintentionally contribute to a disinformation campaign that has, for decades, obscured real human-led assassinations, coverups, and acts of suppression under the veil of “alien menace.”

Let’s be clear: civilizations that master interstellar travel must, by mathematical and philosophical necessity, pass through evolutionary filters that eliminate predatory tendencies. Malice is a short-term survival strategy; cooperation, intelligence, and harmony are long-term imperatives.

Your article also risks delegitimizing the sincere efforts of investigators, scientists, and experiencers who have made meaningful contact, often through peaceful, symbolic, or consciousness-based means.

Moreover, it distracts from genuine human conspiracies—like the suppression of whistleblowers and the murder of individuals (like my grandfather, a California Highway Patrol officer with knowledge of the JFK assassination) who knew too much about earthly, not alien, power.

If there are deaths associated with the UFO field, they are not due to alien hostility. They are the result of human agencies protecting lies with violence.

I invite you, Adriene, to revisit the evidence with fresh eyes, and to consider the ethical implications of our words. Because when we project our own violence onto the stars, we lose the chance to learn from civilizations who chose better.

Sincerely,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Researcher, Physicist, Author of The EFMW Equation
