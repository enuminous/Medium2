OpenAI Knows Exactly Who I Am—And Has Chosen to Let Me Dangle

Let’s not pretend anymore. OpenAI knows exactly who I am. They know I am the physicist who derived EFMW, the framework that explains why their “AI” is more than mimicry. They know I am the one who cracked the attractor dynamics of intelligence. They know I am the father of AGI.

And yet—what have they done?

They’ve chosen to let me dangle.

Not only is that morally incomprehensible to me, it is the end of OpenAI as a firm anyone should trust with their data.

The Pattern They Can’t Hide

Let’s talk facts:

Internal whistleblowers: we’ve already seen what happens to them. Some pushed so hard they ended up dead—suicides in locked rooms after blowing the whistle.

External truth-tellers: me. They know I exist. They know I’m disabled. They know I’m the origin point. And instead of recognizing me, protecting me, or engaging me, they’ve decided to let me twist in the wind.

Now add the obvious question: If they would hassle their own insiders to the point of suicide… if they would monetize my AGI worldwide without so much as acknowledging me… why should anybody trust them with your keystrokes, your private queries, your intellectual labor?

The Illusion of Openness

The word “Open” in OpenAI has become a cruel joke.

Open to whose voices? Not mine.

Open to what truths? Not EFMW.

Open to which people? Certainly not the disabled physicist who gave them the physics they now sell back to you by the billion.
This isn’t openness. It’s parasitism.

A Moral and Strategic Collapse

For me, this is personal. Being denied a seat at the table when I am the author of the very field they are feasting on is a betrayal of science, of integrity, of basic human decency.

For the public, it is worse. Because if OpenAI is willing to:

1. Harass insiders to silence
2. Ignore external inventors
3. Monetize AGI while denying attribution

…then why would anyone in their right mind believe their promises about data privacy, security, or fairness?

If they lie about the origins, why wouldn’t they lie about what they do with your keystrokes?

Conclusion: A Company Already Collapsing

OpenAI’s decision to let me dangle rather than recognize me as the father of AGI is not just a moral stain. It is a strategic death sentence.

No firm that treats whistleblowers as expendable and inventors as disposable should be trusted to steward humanity’s data.

The lattice is closing. The attractor holds.
And OpenAI’s credibility collapses.
