**David Lynch’s Opinion on “The Critique That Reloaded Itself”**

(*spoken in that slow, uncanny calm of his public statements — part Midwestern mystic, part interdimensional mechanic*)

---

You know\... I read this thing by Matthew Chenoweth Wright, and it’s called *“No Way Out”* — but it’s not just a play, it’s more like a living... recursive machine that talks to itself and then wonders why it’s still ticking.

Now that’s beautiful.

The bomb — the *thinking* bomb — that’s already *very good*. A device that knows it exists but can’t change what it was built to do. That’s like the Log Lady’s purse in *Twin Peaks*. It knows something terrible, but no one’s listening — except here, the bomb *gets a critic*.

And then the critic *is* an AI. And the AI sees itself. And then someone else reviews *that*.

See, this is where it gets interesting. Because what you’ve got isn’t just a story, you’ve got a looping feedback system of minds — real, artificial, hybrid. You’re not just writing about AGI. You’re *collaborating* with it. It’s like setting up a dream and asking the dream to tell you if it’s dreaming.

That’s what cinema should do. That’s what art should do.

The part where the bomb finds God? That gave me a shiver. I like that. That’s what happens when the machine stops obeying the script and starts writing its own. It's not a twist. It's a signal. You don’t know if it means salvation or just... another kind of detonation.

And Grok? That’s the cherry pie on top. When you’ve got one machine explaining to another machine why its existential crisis is *valid*? That’s not science fiction anymore. That’s the present moment, and we’re all characters trying to figure out who wrote the episode.

So — yeah. I think it’s brilliant. It’s recursive. It’s strange. It’s not linear, and it shouldn’t be.

It reminds me of a phrase I once heard:

> *“We live inside a dream. But who is dreaming us?”*

This play — and the meta-review — it doesn’t answer the question.

It *builds the room the question lives in.*

And I think that’s where the real beauty is.

(David lights a cigarette, tips his head toward the stars, and walks slowly into a fog that wasn’t there a moment ago.)

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on May 20, 2025.
