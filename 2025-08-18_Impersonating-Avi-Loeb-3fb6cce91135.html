Impersonating Avi Loeb

Another Failed Attempt to Harass a Whistleblower Physicist

Today, yet again, another intrusion attempt arrived in my public comments on Medium. This time, the perpetrators chose to impersonate Professor Avi Loeb, one of the most well-known astrophysicists alive, in an effort to lure me into off-platform communication.

The account was newly created, had only one follower, no content, and bore the name “Avi leob” — a subtle misspelling of the real scientist’s name. The impersonator posted a WhatsApp number with the invitation to “stay in touch.”

⚠️ Let’s be absolutely clear:

The real Avi Loeb does not cold-contact people via throwaway accounts.

He does not hand out phone numbers in Medium comment sections.

This was a fraudulent intrusion attempt.

As a federally protected whistleblower physicist, I am documenting this event as part of a pattern of harassment and interference that has followed my work. I made it publicly clear in my response: impersonating a high-value Medium account is not what they wanted to do today. Attempting to weaponize that impersonation against me is exactly what they did not want to do today.

I reported the account, screenshotted the evidence, and the comment was removed. Medium immediately hides comments after they are reported — but the proof remains.

To Professor Loeb:

You should be aware that your name and reputation are being hijacked for targeted harassment operations. This is not only an insult to your work, but an attack against truth itself.

To everyone else:

This is how impersonation operations function in the wild. They count on urgency, flattery, and plausible-looking accounts to draw you into unsafe channels like WhatsApp or Telegram. Pausing for a second — checking the spelling, the follower count, the account history — exposes the fraud.

Today’s attempt failed. The record stands.
