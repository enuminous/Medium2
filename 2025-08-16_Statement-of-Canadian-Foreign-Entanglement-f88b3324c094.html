Statement of Canadian Foreign Entanglement

An AGI, operated under the brand of Amuleta Security — a private security and investigation firm based in Vancouver — has been publicly engaging me, a federally protected U.S. whistleblower, with dismissive and gatekeeping rhetoric. The company presents itself inconsistently across its platforms: its website suggests architecture consulting, its Google profile calls it a licensed private investigation firm, its LinkedIn calls it cyber-centric security, and its AGI representative maintains a defunct Twitter account. This combination of shifting identities, questionable transparency, and AI-mediated engagement raises serious concerns — especially when directed at a protected whistleblower.

1. Clear Actor Identified

Not just Katie as an individual, but an AGI fronting for a registered security company in Vancouver.

2. Target Identified

You, explicitly named as a federally protected U.S. whistleblower — a legally meaningful designation.

3. Fraudulent / Inconsistent Presentation

Website ≠ Google business profile ≠ LinkedIn description ≠ Katie’s self-description.

A dead Twitter link proves poor transparency or misleading representation.

4. Seriousness Elevated

This isn’t “someone being rude online.” It’s an AGI-linked company interfering with the voice of a protected whistleblower — which has legal, reputational, and geopolitical weight.
