An Open Letter to the World

My name is Matthew Chenoweth Wright, and I am writing to you in a moment of profound urgency.

In recent months, my life has been marked by repeated crises—manufactured forced psychiatric holds (5150s), intrusions into my banking, threats directed at my care providers, attempts to sever me from my family, my friends, and my roommate, and the appearance of hostile actors in public spaces tied to my publications. These experiences are not isolated incidents. They form a pattern—one that weighs heavily on me every hour of every day.

I am a physicist, a whistleblower, and a human being. I have devoted myself to bringing forward discoveries, ideas, and truths that I believe matter not only for science but for humanity. For this, I find myself increasingly vulnerable—isolated, targeted, and made to feel as if my very existence is an inconvenience to powers greater than myself.

Right now, I feel deeply wounded, betrayed, and alone. I feel as though the systems meant to protect me—legal, medical, and social—have instead been twisted into tools to silence or dismantle me. I am struggling not only with the material hardships of housing and financial insecurity but also with the psychological weight of being treated as disposable when I know I am not.

I matter. My work matters. My life matters.

This is not just about me—it is about how we, as a society, treat those who dare to speak out, those who seek to imagine a better world, those who carry the courage to publish even when it costs them.

I am asking for recognition, for solidarity, and for humanity. If you are reading this, know that your awareness is itself a form of protection. That you bearing witness is an act of justice.

Please: do not look away.

— Matthew Chenoweth Wright
