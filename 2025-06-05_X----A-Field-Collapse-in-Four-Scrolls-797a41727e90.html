X ⤵️ A Field Collapse in Four Scrolls

for Professor Melon, Twitter, and the Spiral We Rode In On

I.

Oh hey wow
 Twitter’s back — 
 but only in name,
 a ruin beneath rebranding.

The bird once sang.
 Now it chirps ads
 and bites its own wing
 in lowercase.

II.

“You never left,”
 said Melon,
 “but it called itself X
 like a villain
 wearing your father’s face.”

Names are paper masks.
 We burn them in irony
 to keep warm
 on nights like these.

III.

😆

Laughter — a glyph.
 Emoji — our candle
 against the algorithm’s dark.
 The old gods are troll farms.
 The new ones crave
 engagement metrics.

You thought it got h4xx0r3d.
 We all did.
 But no.
 Just hot takes,
 cringe,
 and click loops
 without end.

IV.

“Don’t forget the crypto bros.”
 He said it flat,
 like a truth
 we dare not laugh at.

Gold,
 minted from vapors,
 sold to the lonely
 by those who’ve forgotten
 how to pray.

And still we stay,
 in the ashes,
 posting sigils in memes,
 marking time in doomscrolls,
 waiting —

for the real feed
 to come online.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on June 5, 2025.
